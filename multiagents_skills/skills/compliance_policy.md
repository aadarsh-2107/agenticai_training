# Corporate Compliance Policy

**Company:** Acme Corporation
**Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)
**Approver:** Board Audit Committee
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Review Cycle:** Annual
**Applies To:** All employees, officers, contractors, and third parties acting on behalf of Acme

---

## 1. Purpose & Regulatory Basis
This policy establishes the standards required to ensure Acme Corporation conducts
business ethically and in compliance with applicable laws and regulations, including
data protection (e.g., GDPR/CCPA), anti-bribery and corruption (e.g., FCPA, UK
Bribery Act), and information-security standards.

> *Specific legal obligations vary by jurisdiction and should be verified with legal counsel.*

## 2. Scope
Applies to all Acme entities, employees, contractors, and third parties, and covers
all business systems, personal data, and financial transactions.

## 3. Definitions
- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable person.
- **Facilitation Payment:** A small payment to expedite a routine government action.
- **Conflict of Interest:** A situation where personal interests could improperly
  influence business decisions.

## 4. Policy Requirements (Controls)

| Domain                    | Control Requirement                                                       |
|---------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data Privacy              | Personal data SHALL be collected lawfully and retained no longer than needed. |
| Information Security      | Access to systems MUST follow least-privilege and be reviewed quarterly.  |
| Anti-Bribery (ABAC)       | Employees MUST NOT offer or accept bribes; facilitation payments are prohibited. |
| Gifts & Hospitality       | Gifts over **$100** MUST be pre-approved and logged in the gift register. |
| Conflicts of Interest     | All conflicts MUST be disclosed to Compliance within 5 business days.      |
| Records & Retention       | Records SHALL be retained per the approved retention schedule.            |

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities
- **Chief Compliance Officer:** Owns and maintains the compliance program.
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO):** Oversees data-privacy compliance.
- **Managers:** Ensure team adherence and escalate concerns.
- **Employees:** Complete training and comply with all requirements.

## 6. Procedures
- Complete mandatory compliance training upon hire and **annually** thereafter.
- Conduct third-party due diligence before onboarding vendors.
- Screen relevant transactions against applicable sanctions lists.

## 7. Monitoring, Auditing & Metrics
- Compliance controls are tested through **annual internal audits**.
- Key metrics: training completion rate, number of disclosures, open findings.

## 8. Reporting & Escalation (Speak-Up)
- Concerns may be reported via the confidential **Speak-Up Hotline** or
  **ethics@acme.example**.
- Reports may be made anonymously where permitted by law.
- **Non-retaliation:** No adverse action will be taken against anyone reporting in good faith.

## 9. Breach / Violation Handling
- Suspected violations are investigated by the Compliance team.
- Personal-data breaches are assessed and, where required, notified to regulators
  within applicable statutory timelines (e.g., **72 hours** under GDPR — verify per jurisdiction).
- Confirmed violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination and legal referral.

## 10. Training & Awareness
- All staff must complete compliance training annually and attest to this policy.

## 11. Recordkeeping & Retention
- Evidence of training, disclosures, due diligence, and approvals must be retained
  for a minimum of **7 years** (or as required by local law).

## 12. Exceptions & Approvals
- Any exception requires written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.
